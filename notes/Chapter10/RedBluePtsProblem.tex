\subsection{An example of strong
induction}\label{an-example-of-strong-induction}}

\textbf{Proposition:} Suppose that \(n\) red dots and \(n\) blue dots
are arranged in the plane so that no three dots lie on a single line.
Then you can draw \(n\) line segments, each connecting one red dot to
one blue dot, in such a way that no two line segments intersect.

\textbf{Proof:} with thanks to
\href{https://puzzling.stackexchange.com/users/10261/john-stevens}{John
Stevens}, and his answer to Puzzling Stack Exchange question number
16643, for the problem and the idea of the proof.
